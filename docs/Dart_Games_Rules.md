***Golf Checkout/Bogey Bash***



**Objective:** complete all selected holes in as few darts as possible.

**Per hole:** each hole has a checkout target and a par. Hit exactly what remains to finish the hole.

**Turn flow**: 3 darts per turn. After each dart, remaining score updates for the current hole.

**Finish a hole:** when remaining reaches 0, the hole is holed/potted and result is tracked against par (Birdie/Par/Bogey etc.).

**If you bust/overshoot:** the dart does not complete the hole; continue within rules until the hole is finished or capped.

**Cap:** each hole has a max dart cap; if reached, the hole closes at cap and play moves on.

**How to win:** after the final hole, lowest total darts (best vs par) wins.

**UI actions:** Use Next to advance after takeout/turn completion.

**Modes:** supports solo and multiplayer. Same rules apply; winner is decided on total performance after all holes.

***Halve-It***



&#x20;The core mechanic is simple: if you fail to hit the target in a round, your entire score is cut in half.

**Setup:** Players start with a base score (usually 0 or 40). A sequence of specific targets is selected before the game (e.g., 12, 13, 14, Any Double, 15, 16, 17, Any Treble, 18, 19, 20, Bull).

**Gameplay:** Each player throws 3 darts per round trying to hit that round's specific target.Hitting the target: Points are scored normally (singles = face value, doubles = 2x, trebles = 3x) and added to your running total.

**Missing the target:** If all 3 darts miss the designated target entirely, your total accumulated score is immediately halved.

**Winning:** The player with the highest score at the end of the final round wins.



***Football/Soccer***



**Setup:** Best played with 2 players or 2 teams.

**Gameplay:**

**Gaining Possession:** To start, a player must first hit the inner bullseye to "take possession" of the ball.

**Scoring Goals:** Once you have possession, you can start shooting at any double on the board. Each double hit counts as 1 goal.

**Losing Possession:** You can keep throwing for doubles until your opponent successfully hits an inner bullseye to "steal" the ball. Once they steal it, it's their turn to shoot for doubles while you try to hit the bullseye to get the ball back.

**Winning:** The first player to reach a set number of goals (usually 10) wins.



***\*Gridiron Darts/American Football***



Players: 2 players or 2 teams (Offense and Defense).

Objective: Advance down a simulated 100-yard field to score touchdowns (7 points) or Field Goals(3 points)

Field: specific grid layout where numbers represent specific plays connected to yardage:



Numbers 1–10: Rushing plays (yards match the number). Doubles and Trebles are 2x and 3x number.



Numbers 11–20: Passing plays (yards match number). Doubles and Trebles are 2x and 3x number.



Bullseye: Touchdown (if on offense) or Interception (if on defense).



Outer Bullseye: Field Goal (offense) or Fumble (Defense)



Misses: Miss outside the Board and yardage goes to 0 for that turn for offense and 5 yard penalty yards for defense



Gaining Yards: Players total yards are compared (offense has 74 yards/Defense has 61 yards). Defense is subtracted from offense to get total yards gained/lossed (74-61=13)



Turnovers: Players on offense have 4 Rounds to gain 10 or more yards for a first down and continue on offense. Players who make it 100 total yards and fail to hit the inner or outer Bull in their 4 Rounds Turn Over The Ball.

***Around The Clock***



**Setup:** Can be played individually or with any number of players.

**Gameplay:** Players take turns throwing 3 darts, attempting to hit the numbers 1 through 20 in exact numerical order.You must hit your current target before you can advance to the next number (e.g., if you are on 1, you cannot advance to 2 until you hit the 1 segment).Usually, doubles and trebles just count as a single hit on that number, though a common house rule allows you to skip ahead if you hit a double (skip 1 number) or treble (skip 2 numbers).

**Winning:** The first player to hit all the numbers in sequence up to 20 (and often finishing on the bullseye) wins.



***Killer***



**Setup:** Each player is randomly assigned or chooses their own number on the board (1-20). Everyone starts with a set number of "lives" (usually 3 or 5).Gameplay:

**Becoming a Killer:** To start the game, you must hit the double of your own assigned number. Once you do, you become a "Killer."

**Eliminating Opponents:** Once you are a Killer, you stop throwing at your own number and start targeting the doubles of your opponents' numbers. Every time you hit an opponent's double, they lose a life.

**Self-Injury:** If a Killer accidentally hits their own double, they lose one of their own lives.

**Winning:** The last player left with any lives remaining wins the game.



***Shanghai***



**Setup:** The game lasts for exactly 7 or 20 rounds, tracking sequentially along the board. In Round 1, the target is 1; Round 2, the target is 2, all the way up to 20.

**Gameplay**: In each round, players throw 3 darts at that round's specific number. Only darts landing in that exact number segment score points (singles, doubles, and trebles score their respective values).

**The "Shanghai" Instant Win:** If a player manages to hit a single, a double, and a treble of the active number all in the same 3-dart turn, they achieve a "Shanghai." The game ends immediately, and that player wins on the spot, regardless of the current scoreboard.

**Winning:** If no one hits a Shanghai by the final round, the player with the highest total accumulated points wins.



***X01 (301 / 501 / 701 / 1001)***



**Core rule:** Start at X01, subtract your score each turn, and finish exactly on 0 with a double.

**Gameplay:** Players alternate turns, throwing three darts per visit. Subtract the total scored from your remaining score.

**Scoring zones:** Single = face value; Double ring = 2× value; Triple ring = 3× value; Outer bull = 25; Inner bull = 50 (counts as a double).

**Finishing (Double-Out Rule):** You must finish on a double (outer ring) or the inner bull (50). Example: 32 must be finished on D16.

**Bust Rule:** A turn is a bust if you score more than your remaining total, you leave 1 remaining (no double finish possible), or you reach 0 without hitting a double. When you bust, your score resets to what it was at the start of the turn.

**Starting Variations:**

- *Straight-In:* Any scoring dart counts immediately.
- *Double-In:* You must hit a double before scoring begins.
- *Master-In:* You must hit a double or triple before scoring begins.

**Finishing Variations:**

- *Straight-Out:* Any scoring dart may finish.
- *Double-Out (standard):* You must finish on a double.
- *Master-Out:* You must finish on a double or triple.

**X01 Formats:**

- *301* — often double-in for the start, but optional.
- *501* — global competitive standard.
- *701* — team play or longer matches.
- *1001* — extended team or advanced training format.

101 through 1001 can be selected as a variation if desired for gameplay.



***Cricket***



**Targets:** Only seven segments matter — 20, 19, 18, 17, 16, 15, and the Bull (25/50). Everything else is dead.

**Marks & Closing:** You must hit each target three times to open it: Single = 1 mark; Double = 2 marks; Triple = 3 marks (instant close); Outer bull = 1 mark; Inner bull = 2 marks. Once you have 3 marks, the number is opened for you. When the opposing player scores 3 marks on that number it closes the number and it becomes dead.

**Scoring:** After you open a number, any additional hits score points equal to the number's value. You can only score while your opponent has NOT closed that number.

**Winning:** To win you must open/close all numbers before your opponent AND have equal or more points than your opponent. If you close everything but trail in points, the game continues until you have more points or the opponent has closed the remaining numbers first.

**Variations:**

- *Standard Cricket:* The classic format described above.
- *Cut-Throat Cricket:* Scoring is reversed — points you score go to your opponents who haven't closed the number. Lowest score wins.
- *No-Score Cricket:* No points are scored at all. First to close all numbers wins.



***Tactics***



Tactics plays exactly like Cricket, but the active target set is expanded.

**Targets:** 20, 19, 18, 17, 16, 15, and the Bull (25/50), plus 14, 13, 12, 11, and 10. Everything else is dead.

**Marks & Closing:** Same as Cricket — hit each target three times to open it: Single = 1 mark; Double = 2 marks; Triple = 3 marks (instant close); Outer bull = 1 mark; Inner bull = 2 marks. Once the opposing player scores 3 marks on a number it closes and becomes dead.

**Scoring:** After you open a number, any additional hits score points equal to the number's value, as long as your opponent has NOT closed that number.

**Winning:** Open/close all numbers (10 through 20 and the Bull) before your opponent AND have equal or more points than your opponent.

**Variations:**

- *Standard Tactics:* The classic scoring format on the expanded target set.
- *Cut-Throat Tactics:* Scoring is reversed — points go to opponents who haven't closed the number; lowest score wins.
- *No-Score Tactics:* No points are scored; first to close all numbers wins.
