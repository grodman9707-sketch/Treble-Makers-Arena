Standard Rules for Cricket and Tactics
Targets:
Only seven segments matter:
20, 19, 18, 17, 16, 15, Bull (25/50)
 [When Tactics is selected the segments also include 14, 13, 12, 11, and 10]  
Everything else is dead.

Marks & Closing:
You must hit each target three times:
Single = 1 mark
Double = 2 marks
Triple = 3 marks (instant close)
Outer bull = 1 mark
Inner bull = 2 marks

Once you have 3 marks, the number is opened for you.
When the opposing player scores 3 marks on that number it closes the number and becomes dead.

Scoring
After you open a number:
Any additional hits score points equal to the number’s value.
You can only score while your opponent has NOT closed that number.

Winning
To win, you must:
Open\Close all numbers before your opponent AND
Have equal or more points than your opponent.
If you close everything but trail in points, the game continues until you have more points or opponent has closed the remaining numbers first.

Variations:
Standard Cricket
The classic format described above.

Cut‑Throat Cricket
Scoring is reversed: points you score go to your opponents who haven’t closed the number.
Lowest score wins.
  
No‑Score Cricket
No points are scored at all.
First to close all numbers wins.
